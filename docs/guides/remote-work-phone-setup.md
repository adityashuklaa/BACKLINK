# Setting Up a Phone System for Remote Teams — The Complete Guide

## The Reality of Remote Business Communication

Your remote team is probably using a mix of personal cell phones, WhatsApp groups, and random Zoom links for business calls. This creates three problems: no call records, unprofessional caller ID, and zero visibility into who talked to which client.

A proper cloud phone system fixes all of this in about 24 hours.

## What You Need

### For Each Remote Employee
- Reliable internet (25 Mbps+ recommended)
- A computer or smartphone (they already have these)
- A headset with noise cancellation ($30-80 investment per person)
- That's it. No desk phones, no hardware, no IT infrastructure.

### For Your Business
- A cloud VoIP provider account
- Your business phone numbers ported to the new system
- 30 minutes to configure call routing

## Step-by-Step Setup

### Step 1: Choose Your Provider (Day 1)
Look for these non-negotiable features:
- **Mobile app** that works on iOS and Android with full calling capability
- **Seamless device switching** — transfer a call from laptop to phone mid-conversation
- **Presence indicators** — see who's available, busy, or offline
- **Business SMS** — text from your business number, not personal
- **Call recording** — automatic, cloud-stored, searchable

[VestaCall](https://vestacall.com) includes all of these in every plan with no per-feature charges.

### Step 2: Port Your Numbers (Day 1-7)
Submit your current phone numbers for porting. This process takes 7-14 business days for local numbers. During porting, your old system stays active — no downtime.

### Step 3: Configure Call Routing (Day 1)
Set up how incoming calls are handled:
- **Main number** → Auto-attendant → Department selection → Ring group
- **Direct lines** → Ring employee's app → Voicemail after 20 seconds
- **After hours** → Voicemail with custom greeting → Email notification

### Step 4: Deploy to Team (Day 2)
Send each employee:
1. Download link for the mobile/desktop app
2. Their login credentials
3. A 10-minute getting-started video
4. Their direct dial number

### Step 5: Go Live (Day 7-14)
Once numbers are ported, all calls automatically route through the new system. Employees answer on their app. Clients notice nothing except maybe better call quality.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Buying desk phones for remote workers** — Waste of money. The app is better.
2. **Skipping the headset** — Laptop mics pick up background noise. A $50 headset makes calls professional.
3. **Not setting up presence** — Without it, people call colleagues who are in meetings.
4. **Ignoring time zones** — Configure business hours per employee, not per company.
