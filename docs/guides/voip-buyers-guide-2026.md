# The Definitive VoIP Buyer's Guide for 2026

## Who This Guide Is For

If you're a business owner, IT manager, or office administrator evaluating phone systems, this guide will save you weeks of research. We've distilled 200+ hours of vendor evaluations into a single resource.

## The Market in 2026

The business phone market has consolidated around three models: legacy landlines (dying), on-premise PBX (shrinking), and cloud VoIP (growing 18% annually). If you're still on a traditional system, you're likely overpaying by 40-60%.

## What to Look For

### Call Quality
HD voice using Opus or G.722 codec is the minimum standard. Ask vendors for a Mean Opinion Score (MOS) — anything below 4.0 is unacceptable for business use. Test during peak hours (10 AM - 2 PM), not during demos scheduled at off-peak times.

### Reliability
Demand a 99.99% uptime SLA with financial credits. That equals less than 53 minutes of downtime per year. Ask how many data centers they operate — single-datacenter providers are a risk.

### Total Cost of Ownership
Don't compare monthly per-user prices. Calculate 3-year TCO including:
- Monthly subscription fees
- Taxes and regulatory surcharges
- Hardware (desk phones, headsets)
- Implementation and training
- Support tier costs

### Security
Non-negotiable requirements:
- TLS 1.3 for signaling encryption
- SRTP for voice encryption
- SOC 2 Type II certification
- Multi-factor authentication for admin portal
